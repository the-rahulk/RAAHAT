Welcome to Maps Section.


Maps


These visual maps show you visually the data of various weather and meteorological anomalies which helps you in tracking certain calamities affecting your area of living. These maps also send you alerts in order to take beforehand action and ensure your safety.


Wind gusts


The wind gusts map show you the data for the sustained wind speeds and wind gusts. Click on your residing area and the map will portray you data regarding specific time, date, the weather of the area, temperature, wind direction, etc. Hurricanes and sudden wind gusts are also visually shown on the map.


Rain accumulation


The rain accumulation map shows the amount of rainwater collection (in mm). Click on your residing area and the map will portray the data regarding the amount of accumulation. The map shows and alerts sudden disasters like flash floods or cloudbursts for your area.


Snow


The snow map shows the precipitation of snow in cm. The visual bar of levels is shown to the bottom. Click on your residing area to get the data regarding the temperature, specific times, etc.


Thunderstorms


Thunderstorms map visually shows the wind speeds and directions and alerts regarding storms hitting your residing area. The coloured bar to the bottom indicates the intensity of the wind. Click on your specific living area to know about data regarding the temperature, wind speeds, direction, etc.


Waves


The waves map shows the data regarding the wave direction. The bar to the bottom of the map indicates the height of the waves. The map indicates the intensity of waves for specific areas and alerts for high waves or tsunamis.


Air Quality


The air quality (CO2 concentration) map shows the visual data of wind direction, CO2 concentrations and its varying intensities over the globe. Click on your specific residing areas to get detailed information about air quality, temperature, wind speeds, wind direction, etc.


Weather warning


The weather warning map shows weather anomalies data over the globe. The bar to the bottom of the map shows the intensities as Moderate, Severe and Extreme. Click on the ' + ' and ' - ' to magnify in and out of the map respectively.
